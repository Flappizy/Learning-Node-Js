World Cup kits: Remembering Africa’s greatest jerseys

Ed Dove
6:50 PM WAT 06/11/2022
GOAL’s Ed Dove ranks Africa’s greatest ever World Cup kits

    #10 Nigeria home (1998)

    An underrated favourite, Nigeria’s ’98 design may get lost in the shuffle sometimes when all-time classic Super Eagles shirts are remembered, but it surely deserves more credit.

    It was a classic Nike design, cutting away with some of the traditional iconography and influences of past and future kits, keeping it simple with a white collar and black and white trim down the flanks and along the shoulders.

    Who can forget the likes of Jay-Jay Okocha, Taribo West and of course, Sunday Oliseh, donning this masterpiece?

    Unfortunately, Nigeria had to wait for their next great World Cup kit, with efforts in 2002 and 2010 not hitting the same heights.

  • Getty Images

    #9 South Africa home (1998)

    Another iconic kit from the 1998 World Cup, this Bafana design maybe wasn’t as classic as the efforts the team wore earlier in the 90s—particularly when winning the Nations Cup in 1996—but it was an effective re-imagined upgrade on that design.

    A vibrant and visually stunning design by Kappa, have Bafana had as strong a kit since?

  • Getty Images

    #8 Ghana away (2010)

    One of several kits in this list that’s associated with a glorious showing at the World Cup, Ghana’s away design in 2010 was the pick of several creative Puma efforts from that tournament…the first on African soil.

    The red and yellow colour scheme was a throwback to some of the great Ghana reverse jerseys from yesteryear, and made the Black Stars look even more imposing than usual

  • Getty

    #7 Nigeria home (1994)

    The first of two Adidas Nigeria designs from the ’94 World Cup, this was a unique and stunning design which marked the Super Eagles’ arrival on the global stage.

  • Getty

    #6 Cameroon home (1990)

    Green shirts, red shorts, yellow socks…Cameroon certainly kept it simple with their 1990 designs, but these were timeless classics, not lease because of the nation’s achievements while wearing these iconic Adidas designs.

    The white Adidas stripes on the shoulders modernised Cameroon’s classic look, while the roaring lion over the players’ hearts made these Lions truly Indomitable.

  • Getty Images

    #5 Algeria away (1982)

    Algeria’s campaign at the 1982 was overshadowed by the collaboration between West Germany and Austria, as the pair combined to play out a mutually beneficial result that eliminated the North Africans.

    However, Algeria did themselves proud with some excellent displays, while also being arguably the best dressed team in Spain.

    They even switched their kits during the upset of West Germany, sporting two classic designs, each of which wrote out ‘al-Jaza’ir’ ---Algeria in Arabic—on the front.

    Loud and proud from the Fennecs here.

  • #4 Nigeria away (1994)

    The second of the two Nigeria designs from the 1994 World Cup, this unique pattered design would go on to prove the inspiration for Nigeria’s iconic 2018 jersey.

    The image of Rashidi Yekini, sporting this design, clutching the goal net in delight after scoring against Bulgaria, is one of the iconic moments in African football history.

  • Getty Images / Puma

    #3 Cameroon (2002)

    An unforgettable kit in the all-time pantheon of classic football shirts, Cameroon and Puma delivered a masterpiece with their 2002 collection.

    Of course, going for a vest design was always going to ruffle a few feathers, but even when that approached was nixed by the authorities, the manufacturers still adapted in style.

    Wearing black t-shirts underneath meant that the original concept was largely maintained, with the white away kit a similarly effective design.

    This kit was followed up by the infamous one-piece design, ahead of the 2004 Africa Cup of Nations.

  • Getty Images / Nike

    #2 Nigeria home (2018)

    A record-breaking design from Nike, Nigeria’s kit for the 2018 World Cup was an instant classic and represents one of the most striking designs ever seen at the Mondial.

    With three million pre-orders, this kit was already famous before it had been worn once, and proved to be a genuine piece of art, with a retro design that celebrated African tradition while also appearing futuristic.

    A captivating design, that could have been a worthy winner of this ranking…

  • Goal

    #1 Zaire home (1974)

    In contrast to Zaire’s ignominious and ill-fated campaign at the 1974 World Cup—which included a 9-0 tonking by Yugoslavia—their kits remain the standard by which all future African kits have been judged.

    The Congo’s design was a timeless classic, with the bold yellow Adidas sleeves and the roaring leopard’s face on the front of the shirt immediately announcing Sub-Saharan Africa’s arrival on the world stage.

    It’s just such a shame that the team weren’t able to do this magnificent design justice.
